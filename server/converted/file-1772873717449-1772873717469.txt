Introduction:
IBM (International Business Machines) is known for its hardware and software products, including
computers, servers, storage systems and networking equipment. IBM corporation is a global IT and outsourcing
company.
In the digital era, it is common for organizations to have multiple clouds for different and varying
workloads. Big Blue, which has over several decades watched and led the transformation of the IT industry, is
now looking to tap into the huge unexplored hybrid cloud market which it believes is the next big opportunity.
IBM calls this as the chapter 2 of the cloud. While chapter 1 of the cloud was largely about moving a lot of new
and customers facing application to the cloud, chapter 2 of the c;oud is the US $1.2 trillion hybrid cloud market.
IBM INDIA:
IBM INDIA, “cloud 1.0 in India was all about agility, scalability, time and cost savings. As businesses
started down the path towards digitalization, it assisted them in being more nimble and automating procedures.
Cloud 2.0 focuses more on leveraging the cloud's potential and the latest developments in digital transformation
to support businesses in scaling and experiencing exponential growth. Cloud 2.0 focuses on modernization,
administration, infrastructure, security, governance, and seamless integration, building on the agility and cost
reductions provided by Cloud 1.0. India is leading the way in cloud adoption, with enterprises understanding its
potential to help optimize current business systems, re-architect them, modernize them, and provide new
business services at previously unthinkable speeds and levels of innovation.
Bajwa claims that IBM has positioned itself advantageously in the increasingly profitable hybrid cloud industry
thanks to his company's approach of customising goods and services to customers' requests rather than what
suppliers are attempting to offer. According to him, "We are always improving our cloud platform to make it
easier for businesses to connect to the cloud by meeting their needs at every stage of the process, from speeding
up the migration of current workloads to the cloud to modernising and expanding current apps to providing the
necessary tools to create cloud-native apps from the ground up." Thanks to this tactic, IBM Cloud is now the
entry point for blockchain, IoT, AI, and even cutting-edge technologies like quantum computing.

AQUISITION OF RED HAT – A GAME CHANGER:
Red Hat's open source innovation is now available to a wider spectrum of clients thanks to the purchase,
which has changed the game. Businesses can get the hybrid cloud solutions they need from IBM and Red Hat,
which are built on open source technologies like Linux and Kubernetes and give real choice and agility. Bajwa
explains, "Hybrid multicloud environments will be based on a foundation of 'open' as clients will look at securely
deploying, running, and managing their mission-critical workloads across on-premises, private, and public cloud
environment." With the top-ranked Linux operating system and the industry-leading hybrid cloud platform,
OpenShift, Red Hat is at the core of this. We are in a good position to combine these with IBM's enterprise
incumbency, scale, and expertise.
Forrester Consulting was recently hired by IBM as part of developing "Total Economic Impact"
research, which aims to investigate how customers use products from both businesses simultaneously and the
resulting impact. These firms implemented a broad range of Red Hat (Red Hat Enterprise Linux, OpenShift, JBoss,
and professional services) and IBM (IBM Power, IBM Z, IBM Cloud Paks, middleware, and professional
services) solutions. According to the report, businesses boosted release frequency by up to 10 times, sped up
development cycles by up to 66%, and cut infrastructure hardware and licencing expenses by as much as 50%.
With the acquisition of Red Hat, IBM gains a significant edge as open technologies like containers and
Kubernetes become the common fabric for both creating cloud-native applications and modernising legacy
programmers. To run on Red Hat OpenShift, for instance, IBM has optimised over 100 items from its vast
software portfolio. Now that Red Hat OpenShift has been optimised for IBM software and new Cloud Paks,
organizations may develop mission-critical apps only once and deploy them across all private and public clouds.
AI POWER:
IBM aspires to be the nation's face of artificial intelligence. According to Viswanath Ramaswamy
(Vishy), Director of Systems at IBM South Asia and India, "We are doing to AI what Red Hat did to Linux."
With Power AI, you may select any AI framework you want. Everybody's AI path is different at this point.
In support of his argument, Ramaswamy cites the example of IFFCO Tokio General Insurance, which
analyses the extent of the damage to personal cars using IBM Power9 and the optimized DL frameworks that

damaged in the accident and are both repairable and replaceable. In a short while, the evaluation and the price are
